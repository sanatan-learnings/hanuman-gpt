- **рдирд╛рд╕реИ** (Nasai) - destroy/cure

- **рдкреАрд░рд╛** (Pira) - pain/suffering
- **рдЬрдкрдд** (Japat) - chanting/repeating
- **рдирд┐рд░рдВрддрд░** (Nirantar) - continuously/constantly
- **рд╣рдиреБрдордд** (Hanumat) - of Hanuman
- **рдмреАрд░рд╛** (Bira) - heroic name

## 5. Literal Translation

Diseases are destroyed and all pain is removed by continuously chanting the name of the heroic Hanuman.

## 6. Interpretive Meaning

This verse promises healing - physical, mental, and spiritual. "Nirantar japat" (continuous chanting) emphasizes regularity and devotion, not just mechanical repetition. The removal of "sab pira" (all suffering) includes not just physical illness but emotional and spiritual pain. This makes Hanuman a complete healer.

## 7. Story Behind the Verse

Hanuman is known as Sankat Mochan (remover of difficulties) and is particularly associated with healing. When Lakshmana was dying, Hanuman brought the life-saving herb. This healing aspect has made Hanuman especially dear to those facing health challenges. Many devotees report relief from chronic conditions through sincere Hanuman worship.

## 9. Practical Application

**Key Teaching:**
Continuous, sincere chanting of Hanuman's name brings healing to body, mind, and spirit - removing all forms of suffering.

**When to Use This Verse:**
Recite this verse when dealing with illness, chronic pain, or any form of physical, emotional, or spiritual suffering.
