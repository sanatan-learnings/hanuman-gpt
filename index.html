About the Hanuman Chalisa

The Hanuman Chalisa is a devotional hymn dedicated to Lord Hanuman, composed by the 16th-century poet-saint Goswami Tulsidas. Written in Awadhi language, it consists of 40 verses (chaupais) preceded by two introductory dohas (couplets) and followed by a closing doha.

The word "Chalisa" means "forty," referring to the 40 verses that extol Hanuman's virtues, strength, and unwavering devotion to Lord Rama. Regular recitation of the Hanuman Chalisa is believed to bring strength, courage, and protection from negative energies.
